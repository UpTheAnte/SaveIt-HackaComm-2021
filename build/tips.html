How does saving electricity benefit you and the world around you?

How can you save electricity in your daily life?

Why should you save?

Even as the world moves towards renewable energy sources, it will take a long time to fully switch away from fossil fuels. Burning fossil fuels emits gases which causes global warming. Gases such as methane, chlorofluorocarbons, and carbon dioxide in our atmosphere regulate the earthâ€™s temperature by trapping heat. Additionally, electricity costs money, and reducing your electricity usage also helps you save on your electricity bill.

How do you save?

There are several ways you can save electricity, some more effective than others. Here are a few easy tips to help you save electricity:

  1. Unplug Devices

    2. This is one of the more obvious ones. Even when your devices are on standby (ex. your computer is sleeping), they're each using a little bit of electricity while waiting for you to use them. Even if it's not a lot of electricity, multiple devices plugged in for long periods of time will cause the electricity to add up.

  2. Air dry

    3. Instead of drying your clothes in the dryer, hang them to dry in the sunlight, but make sure that you don't slip on the floor after. Similarly, instead of using the dishwasher's drying function, dry them with a towel or leave them in the dishwasher to air dry.

  3. Get a Smart Thermostat

    4. Most people aren't home during the daytime on the weekdays because they're at work or school. However, it isn't enjoyable to arrive home and find it to be freezing or boiling. Therefore, many people will keep the heat or air conditioning on throughout the day. This uses a lot of electricity, but it can be easily fixed with a smart thermostat, which allows you to set different temperatures for your home during different times of day. You could turn off the thermostat for most of the day, and change the temperature when you're almost at home.


  4. Adjust lights

    5. By changing the number of lights you have in a room, you might be able to save some electricity. For example, use one 100 watt lightbulb in place of 2-3 60 watt lightbulbs to save some energy.
